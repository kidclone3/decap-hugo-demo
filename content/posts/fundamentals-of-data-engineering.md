> Reis, Joe; Housley, Matt. *Fundamentals of Data Engineering*. 1st ed., O'Reilly, 2022. ISBN 9781098108250.

## Source Guide

This comprehensive text serves as a definitive guide to the modern **data engineering lifecycle**, positioning the field as a mature, independent discipline essential for the success of data science and analytics. The authors define data engineering as a holistic process of **developing and maintaining systems** that transform raw data into high-quality information, moving beyond a simple focus on specific tools to emphasize **underlying principles** that stand the test of time.

Central to the source is a five-stage framework — **generation, storage, ingestion, transformation, and serving** — which describes the "cradle to grave" journey of data. Supporting these stages are several critical **undercurrents**, including **security, data management, and DataOps**, which ensure that systems remain robust, ethical, and aligned with business goals.

Ultimately, the text illustrates how the role has evolved from low-level infrastructure management toward high-level **lifecycle orchestration**, requiring practitioners to balance technical proficiency with **strategic business responsibilities**.
